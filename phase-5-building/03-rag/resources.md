# Resources: RAG

Curated external resources for going deeper on Retrieval-Augmented Generation.

---

## Reference code

**[LangChain — RAG from scratch](https://github.com/langchain-ai/rag-from-scratch)**
https://github.com/langchain-ai/rag-from-scratch
The most comprehensive code-first breakdown of every RAG component. Covers basic retrieval, query transformation, re-ranking, and advanced techniques like RAPTOR and CRAG. The notebooks are provider-agnostic in their concepts even when they use specific libraries.

**[LlamaIndex](https://github.com/run-llama/llama_index)**
https://github.com/run-llama/llama_index
The other major RAG framework. Useful as a reference for the range of chunking, indexing, and retrieval patterns that exist — even if you implement them from scratch. Their documentation explains why each design choice was made.

---

## Blog posts

**[Pinecone — What is RAG?](https://www.pinecone.io/learn/retrieval-augmented-generation/)**
https://www.pinecone.io/learn/retrieval-augmented-generation/
A thorough, visual explanation of the full RAG pipeline by the team that builds one of the leading vector databases. Covers the problem RAG solves, the architecture, and common pitfalls — without assuming prior knowledge.

**[Pinecone — Chunking strategies](https://www.pinecone.io/learn/chunking-strategies/)**
https://www.pinecone.io/learn/chunking-strategies/
A practical comparison of fixed-size, sentence-based, semantic, and recursive chunking with benchmarks on retrieval quality. The most useful single reference for deciding which chunking approach to use.
