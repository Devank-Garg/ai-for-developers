# Resources: Risks & Society

None required — the concepts file covers everything needed for this module. These are for going deeper.

---

## Essential watching

**"Do Language Models Know What They Don't Know?" — Jacob Steinhardt (CHAI)**
A grounded technical researcher talking about what near-term AI risks actually are, as distinct from speculative long-term concerns. Useful for calibrating the discussion.

**"The Social Dilemma" (Netflix, 2020)**
Covers recommendation system dynamics, engagement optimisation, and misinformation amplification. Not specifically about LLMs but directly relevant to AI-at-scale societal risks. Watch the first 45 minutes at minimum.

**"AI and the Future" — Yann LeCun vs. Yoshua Bengio (various debates)**
Find any recent public exchange between LeCun and a safety-concerned researcher. Seeing the actual arguments made in real time is more useful than any summary.

---

## Illustrated guides

**"Moral Responsibility in the Age of AI" — MIT Technology Review**
Covers the developer responsibility question with concrete case studies. Accessible and developer-oriented.

**"The Atlas of AI" — Kate Crawford**
A book, but the author's talk versions are available online. Covers the environmental, labour, and political economy of AI infrastructure. A necessary counterpoint to capability-focused narratives.

---

## Conceptual reading

**"Weapons of Math Destruction" — Cathy O'Neil (2016)**
Pre-ChatGPT but directly applicable: a data scientist documenting how algorithmic systems encode and amplify discrimination. Still the clearest book-length treatment of bias in consequential AI.

**"The Alignment Problem" — Brian Christian (2020)**
Developer-friendly treatment of the full range of AI safety concerns, from near-term bias to long-term alignment. Narrative non-fiction, not a textbook.

**"Stochastic Parrots" — Bender, Gebru et al. (2021)**
The paper that examined the risks of large language models from a data, bias, and environmental perspective. Read the abstract and Sections 5–6 (environmental cost and bias). This paper led to the departure of Timnit Gebru from Google; the backstory is itself illustrative.

**"Situational Awareness" — Leopold Aschenbrenner (2024)**
A long essay from a former OpenAI safety researcher arguing that AGI is closer than most people believe and that the geopolitical implications are severe. Read it alongside critical responses. Represents one serious technical perspective.

**"Benefits and Burdens of AI" — Daron Acemoglu (Foreign Affairs, 2023)**
Acemoglu's economic analysis of who AI benefits. Sceptical of broad prosperity claims; grounded in labour economics data.

## Practical reference

**The C2PA Standard (c2pa.org)**
The technical specification for content provenance standards. Understanding what it actually does (and doesn't) is more useful than reading media coverage of it.

**Algorithmic Justice League (ajl.org)**
Joy Buolamwini's organisation documenting facial recognition bias. Her research ("Gender Shades") is the source for much of the accuracy disparity data in this module.

**AI Now Institute (ainowinstitute.org)**
Annual reports on the societal impact of AI in specific sectors (employment, healthcare, criminal justice). Evidence-based and developer-relevant.

**Future of Life Institute — AI Safety Resources (futureoflife.org)**
Covers the existential risk perspective. Read their materials alongside critical responses from AI researchers who disagree.

**Electronic Frontier Foundation (eff.org/ai)**
Covers surveillance, privacy, and civil liberties in AI from a rights-focused perspective. Useful counterbalance to capability-focused industry coverage.
